Source Quality Logistic Regression

From the data manipulation above and the data visulization we did before, we decide to work on the problem that how other variables contribuate to the “Source Quality”. From EDA, there are some points have to be noticed, most variables are not quantifiable, so we have to convert them to dummy variables to build our model. For the “Source Quality”, I decide to distinguish them to 0(<=3) and 1(>3) and 0 for unreliable and 1 for reliable. For the “Region of Incident”, we create 15 dummy variables instead of one column. For the “URL”, since there are more than 1000 unique links which are hard to deal with, we convert it to 0 and 1, 0 means website is unaviliable and 1 means website is aviliable. Before model building, I want to check whether our data is oversampled. With our training data Created, I up-sampled the data with SMOTE algorithm. Then we can have a perfect balanced data. After that, I use Recursive Feature Elimination (RFE) to select features. And the result showed our variables are good to use. At last, I build two model: the first is Logistic Regression and second is Neural Network. Based on my experience, I guess around so many statistics models and algorithm models, these two may perform better. The logistic regression model performed very well, it has an 83% accuracy. And I tried different layers, units and tuned different parameters in the NN model, the best accuray is 61%, otherwise it would be overfitting or inaccurate. And compared to other models that Hemani built, the logistic regression model still is the best. From the result, we can tell that most of our variables contributes a lot to the reliablity under the 0.05 significance level. P-value of “Central Asia” and “North American” is large, this may means reliablity of incidents happened here may hard to be tell.
